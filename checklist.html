Your Emergency Checklist

Prepare your emergency kit in advance. A well-stocked kit can make all the difference when a disaster strikes. Use this guide to help you get ready.

Water & Food Supplies

Stock up on essentials for at least 3 days. Aim for a gallon of water per person, per day.

  • Water (1 gallon per person, per day)
  • Non-perishable food
  • Manual can opener
  • Food for pets

First Aid & Health

Assemble a comprehensive first aid kit with supplies to handle common injuries and medical needs.

  • First aid manual
  • Bandages, gauze, and tape
  • Prescription medication (7-day supply)
  • Personal hygiene items

Tools & Emergency Supplies

Essential tools for light, communication, and basic repairs that can be critical in an emergency situation.

  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio
  • Whistle to signal for help
  • Wrench or pliers

Documents & Money

Keep copies of important documents and cash in a waterproof bag for easy access and security.

  • Cash in small bills
  • Copies of IDs, passports, and insurance
  • List of emergency contacts
  • Local map

Sanitation & Hygiene

Maintaining hygiene is crucial to prevent the spread of illness during and after a disaster event.

  • Wet wipes, hand sanitizer
  • Garbage bags and plastic ties
  • Dust mask
  • Feminine hygiene products

Clothing & Bedding

Pack a change of clothes and blankets to stay warm and comfortable for at least a few days.

  • Complete change of clothes for each person
  • Sleeping bag or warm blanket
  • Rain gear
  • Sturdy shoes
