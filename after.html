After a Disaster

The first 72 hours after a disaster are critical. Here's a guide to navigating the aftermath and beginning the process of recovery.

Immediate Actions for Your Safety

1. Stay Safe

Check for injuries, and if it is safe to do so, help others. Turn on your radio for official instructions and avoid damaged areas. Stay away from downed power lines and damaged structures.

2. Contact Family

Use text messages or social media to contact family and friends. Phone lines may be overwhelmed, and text messages often get through more reliably.

3. Document Damage

Take photos and videos of any damage to your property for insurance purposes. Do this from a safe distance before beginning any cleanup.

4. Stay Informed

Listen to local radio or official news sources for information on shelters, aid centers, and recovery efforts. Avoid unofficial social media for critical information.

Understanding official guidance is crucial. Authorities will provide details on safe areas, distribution points for food and water, and updates on infrastructure.
