The Complete Disaster Preparedness Guide

Preparation, swift action, and a plan for recovery are your most powerful tools in any emergency. This guide provides a comprehensive framework to help you and your family navigate a crisis.

Before: Anticipate & Prepare

Build an Emergency Kit

Prepare a kit with essential supplies that can sustain you and your family for at least 72 hours.

  • Water (one gallon per person per day)
  • Non-perishable food
  • First-aid kit and medications
  • Flashlight and extra batteries
  • Battery-powered radio
  • Important documents and cash

Create a Communication Plan

Decide on a meeting place and a family contact outside of your local area. Text messages often work when phone calls don't.

  • Designate an out-of-state contact
  • Establish a primary and secondary meeting place
  • Make a list of emergency numbers
  • Include important personal information

During: Respond & Stay Safe

Follow Official Instructions

Listen to local authorities and emergency broadcasters. Evacuate immediately if instructed to do so.

  • Tune into a battery-powered radio
  • Follow designated evacuation routes
  • Use text messages for communication

Shelter in Place Safely

If advised to stay indoors, find the safest room in your home, away from windows and doors.

  • Close all doors and windows
  • Shut off utilities if necessary
  • Stay together as a family

After: Recover & Rebuild

Check on Yourself and Others

Assess injuries and provide first aid. Check on neighbors and family members who may need assistance.

  • Administer first aid for minor injuries
  • Contact out-of-state family to let them know you are safe
  • Look for and report any immediate hazards

Assess Damage and Begin Recovery

Safely inspect your property for damage. Document all damage for insurance purposes.

  • Take photos or videos of all damage
  • Contact your insurance company
  • Begin cleanup and temporary repairs only when it is safe to do so
