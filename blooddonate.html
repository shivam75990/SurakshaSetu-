Your Donation Can Save a Life

Donating blood is a simple act that can have a profound impact. Learn why it matters and how you can help.

Why Your Donation is Critical

Emergency Situations

Blood is vital for trauma victims, surgical patients, and people in critical condition after a disaster.

Ongoing Patient Care

Patients with chronic illnesses, cancer, or blood disorders require regular transfusions to survive.

A Constant Need

Blood cannot be manufactured, so the only source is from generous donors. The supply must be constantly replenished.

Are You Eligible?

Most healthy individuals can donate blood. Here are the general requirements:

  • You must be in good general health.
  • You must be at least 16 or 17 years old, depending on local regulations.
  • You must weigh at least 110 lbs (50 kg).
  • You must wait a certain period between donations (usually 56 days for whole blood).

Not Sure?

Many people who think they can't donate are actually eligible. It's always best to check with a local donation center for specific guidelines.

Where to Donate

Blood donation centers are located in communities across the country. Find a center or blood drive near you.
